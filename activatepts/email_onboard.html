Welcome to the NeCTAR Research Cloud

Hi there,

Your project trial with NeCTAR is now active, so enjoy having a look around! You will also get an email from NeCTAR with some additional information, but we thought we'd share some UoA-specific information that might also be helpful as you get started.

    Where are my instances and data stored?
  • By default, your instances and all of your data is stored within UoA data centres
  • You can make services of yours (like a web portal) open to the world. Please use common sense with respect to security, and reach out if you're not sure-- we'll be happy to help

    • How can I get support?
  • NeCTAR is largely a self-service environment, but we suppport the platform and do our very best to help with anything else.

    • To get support:
    • Click the "lifesaver" ring at the top of the dashboard to create a support ticket
    • Email nectarsupport@auckland.ac.nz
    • Stop by Hacky Hour at the Strata Postgraduate Cafe in Kate Edgar Commons on Thursdays at 3pm

    How can I get more resources or use NeCTAR for longer-term?
  • At any point in the trial, you can apply for a full allocation by submitting an allocation request, right from the dashboard (Allocations tab > New Request). Just do your best filling it out— we can adjust later as needed. For the use case field, paste in an abstract describing your research in some detail.
  • Please include an abstract in your allocation request (in the Research Use Case field), that describes in some detail what you're researching
  • NeCTAR Projects are approved for up to a year. This is renewable.
  • If your application is approved, you'll be asked to take part in a short survey at the end of the year; this is really important to maintaining our funding for NeCTAR.
  • We have many services we can offer you-- just ask and we're happy to have a chat.


    • Example Services:
    • Volume storage for your data
    • GPUs (reservable in 3 month blocks at the moment)
    • Advanced self-service networking
    • DNS (assign a host name to your instance)
    • Windows images (ask us)
    • Access to Virtual Labs

  • NeCTAR gives you a lot of freedom to do manage things yourself, but right now won't be able to access some UoA services, like: Unifiles, your department shared drive, standard UoA/SSO login, printing, etc. We do offer a more managed VM service that does have access to these features. There's more information about this service here: https://research-hub.auckland.ac.nz/#/content/1
